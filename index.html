SHARING MY ACTIVITY

My current activity is as a librarian in a village library, if you want to know something, the library as a treasure trove of knowledge plays an important role in order to educate and increase public knowledge, therefore I chose to work temporarily as a librarian, the existence of libraries in the midst of society, schools, colleges, universities, or in other institutions has a very big impact on the progress where the library is located. the issue of growing and increasing the spirit of reading and making reading a culture of Indonesian society is one of the most interesting issues to discuss. This is because the growth of the spirit of reading can lead to the ideal point of a nation. An ideal nation is a noble ideal of a nation because an ideal nation is a characteristic of an advanced and civilized nation so that the right way is needed to bring it to an ideal condition. One of these basic keys is by reading, because someone who likes to read must have ideas, and if they have ideas, they are followed by efforts to build those ideas into reality. In the era of development and advancement of science and technology today, the role of libraries and information centers is increasingly felt by the community as a source of development information as well as a means of learning and teaching to improve intelligence and skills. In addition, libraries play a role as non-formal educational institutions that can provide opportunities for lifelong education. Books as a mass communication tool are a very important thing in spurring people's interest in reading. Reading is the most basic activity in education and is one of the most important skills and habits in everyday life. Through reading, people can find new ideas to get information, increase their knowledge and broaden their horizons

But the main problem in Indonesia is actually not alliteration (illiteracy), because the number is getting smaller and smaller. Our main problem is actually those who can read, but do not want to read. In information science, the unwillingness to read is called alliteration. This problem occurs because there is no motivation or motivator to read and an environment that does not read when people can read. It is not difficult to find the roots, because it is often discussed, among others, the strong culture of hearing and oral culture, the socio-economic conditions of society do not support interest in reading and people's purchasing power, technological and communication advances, especially electronic media, can be a threat to interest in reading, and the learning/teaching system and curriculum in schools or universities do not support the love of reading and writing. Indeed, theoretically there is a positive relationship between reading interest and reading habits and reading ability. The low interest in reading in society results in low reading habits, and these low reading habits also result in low reading ability. That is what is happening in Indonesian society today. Reading is indeed more difficult when compared to seeing or hearing. Reading requires the ability to understand a series of sentences and then interpret them yourself without the help of others, and not everyone has enough patience to do something like that. The culture of reading in our society is still low and concerning. We are still sucked into the tradition of hearing and speaking, not yet shifting to an advanced society that likes to read and write. Not only farmers, fishermen, laborers, small businessmen, and lower-class people in villages but academics, bureaucrats, and ordinary people in the upper middle class are also lazy to read and write
